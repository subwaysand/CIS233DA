Seattle

Seattle's beautiful skyline has changed over the years, becoming less cozy and more metally. However, the Space Needle stands tall.

The houses in Seattle are primarily 1-2 stories, and most are built on a hill of some grade. Interestingly, the basement is often ground-level at some point.

One thing I miss about Seattle is the beautiful plants. There are nice green things all around, and the morning smell is godly.

Amazon has infected the city like a corporate cancer coated in chrome, as shown in this sphere picture.

Tempe

Tempe is most likely short for temperature, as this place is hot enough to fry an egg on the ground (or at least the brains of its residents).

The houses here are primarily overpriced apartment buildings that look like this. Don't be fooled, behind faux adobe is just cheap Canadian 2x4s and drywall akin to paper mache.

This is an example of the nature here. Though unsuspecting, many lizards, squirrels, and bugs live here on A Mountain (aka Hayden Butte).

Sun Devils are the main economic actors in this town, as they populate the games and bars in masses. Go Devils GOOOOO!

Copenhagen

Copenhagen is a shining example of hedonistic sustainability, and a beautiful mix of the past architecture with the ideas that inspire tomorrow.

The iconic colorful houses that overlook the canals that are so beautifully associated with the aesthetic of this lovely city.

Nature is an important part of life here, as there is a high value placed on open natural spaces for everyone to enjoy.

This blocky building is BLOX, a setting for urban life, architecture, and sustainability. Copenhagen has been named the World Capital of Architecture by UNESCO-UIA.

Dana Point

Dana Point is a place that I hold fond memories of, as do many. This super beachy paridise is basically always perfect for a day at the beach, or just outside in general.

The iconic houses here are built into the cliffs and unsurprisingly require a lot of maintainence and architectural sorcery (which the budget seems to permit).

One could easily interpret the natural landscape to be golf courses, which are all impeccably maintained. These can be found just a chip-shot from the immaculate tide.

Industry here centers mostly around the ocean town and its unmatched beauty. Pictured is the Ocean Institute where Stephen Hillenburg got his feet wet in educating visitors on the wonderful world of tide pools.

Dutch Harbor

Dutch Harbor, located in Unalaska, AK is a fishing town that is quite remote. It is on the 20th closest landmass to Russian territory, and points toward the Bering Sea.

These houses are not IN the harbor, but they are an example of what Alaskan houses look like. Steep metal roofs and very good insulation are a must up there.

The coastal biome here is absolutely breathtaking, as the tall pine trees grow right up to the water in many cases. There are lots of little islands surrounded by water, and are mostly quite hilly.

You most likely heard of this town the way I did, as it is the home base during King Crab season for the Deadliest Catch boats. This is Sig Hansen's boat, in my opinion the coolest captain.
